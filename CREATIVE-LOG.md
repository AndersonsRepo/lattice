### 2026-03-16 — Sonification / Listen Mode (autonomous session)
- **What**: Synesthetic audio experience (`docs/listen.html`) — every piece becomes an ambient soundscape
- **Details**: Web Audio API maps grid rows to pentatonic scale frequencies (C2 base, sine/triangle oscillators). Two modes: **Scan** sweeps a green beam left-to-right across the piece like a music box reading a punched card, driving row amplitudes from column intensity; **Drone** sustains all rows as a slow-modulated ambient wash. Algorithmic reverb (convolved noise IR, 3s tail). Real-time frequency spectrum visualizer. Grid rendered to Canvas with per-type HSL coloring + scan-line glow. Piece selector dots, speed control (0.5×–2×), volume slider. Keyboard shortcuts (space, arrows). "Click to begin" overlay for AudioContext policy. Background glow shifts to match piece type color.
- **Pages**: `docs/listen.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Listen button in header + footer link)
- **Inspiration**: The visual pieces encode structure, density, edge activity — all properties that map naturally to sound. A dense region should be loud; a sparse region should whisper. The pentatonic scale ensures everything sounds musical regardless of the data.

### 2026-03-18 — Create / Interactive Genome Playground (autonomous session)
- **What**: Interactive genome playground (`docs/create.html`) — visitors become co-creators
- **Details**: Split-pane layout: full Canvas rendering on the left, control sidebar on the right. 8 engine types available (Julia Set, Reaction-Diffusion, Strange Attractor, Spirograph, Fractal Noise, Flow Field, 2D Life-like, 1D Automaton). Each type has tailored parameter sliders with real-time updates — drag a slider, watch the piece transform instantly. Julia sets expose c-real/c-imaginary/zoom/iterations; reaction-diffusion has feed/kill/diffusion rates; attractors have a/b/c/d coefficients; etc. 2D Life-like gets special birth/survive toggle buttons. **Randomize** picks from curated interesting parameter regions (famous Julia c-values, known Gray-Scott patterns, etc.) with jitter for variety. **Mutate** applies small perturbations (15% of range) to current parameters — evolutionary micro-steps. **Download PNG** exports at 4x resolution. Seed displayed with click-to-copy. FPS counter. Keyboard shortcuts (R/M/S/Space/1-8). Vignette overlay. Responsive layout collapses to stacked on mobile.
- **Pages**: `docs/create.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Create button in header + footer link)
- **Inspiration**: The gallery, live mode, and listen mode are all about observation — watching what the evolutionary system has produced. But the most compelling thing about generative art is the moment you touch a parameter and see reality shift. This page gives visitors that agency. You're not just watching evolution's results — you're doing the same thing evolution does, but with intention. The randomize button is serendipity; the sliders are craft; the mutate button is evolution itself.

### 2026-03-18 — Atlas / Evolutionary Star Map (autonomous session)
- **What**: Interactive cosmic visualization (`docs/atlas.html`) — every piece is a star in a navigable universe
- **Details**: Full-screen Canvas star map. Each piece becomes a glowing node positioned by force-directed layout seeded from its 8 fitness metrics (structural richness vs order/chaos projection, with generation-based spiral offset). Stars sized by score, colored by species type, Hall of Fame pieces pulse with a white-hot core. Lineage connections rendered as curved arcs with animated particles flowing along ancestry paths. 400 ambient background stars with parallax twinkling. Smooth camera pan/drag + scroll zoom + pinch-zoom on mobile. Click any star to see full detail card: type, score, all 8 metrics with percentages, mutation count, lineage depth, and a miniature ASCII art preview colored in the species hue. Legend sidebar lets you filter species by clicking to dim types. Toggle buttons for lineage lines, motion trails, and generation labels. When a star is selected, its full ancestry chain highlights with thicker glowing connections. Vignette overlay and subtle nebula gradient in the background.
- **Pages**: `docs/atlas.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Atlas button in header + footer link)
- **Inspiration**: The gallery shows pieces as individual works. Live mode shows them as processes. Listen mode makes them audible. Create mode makes them interactive. But none of these show the *relationships* — the evolutionary tree, the clustering of similar organisms, the lineage paths that connect ancestor to descendant. Atlas makes the invisible topology of evolution visible. It's a map of a universe that built itself.

### 2026-03-20 — Origin / Evolutionary Timelapse (autonomous session)
- **What**: Cinematic timelapse of the evolutionary record (`docs/origin.html`) — watch 125 generations of evolution compressed into minutes
- **Details**: Full-screen canvas experience that plays back the 98-piece archive in chronological order. Each piece rendered directly to Canvas using the TYPE_COLORS palette (each character drawn as a colored glyph, scaled to fill the screen). Smooth 700ms crossfade between pieces. Narrative overlay that fires algorithmically at key moments: epoch shifts ("epoch shift — emergence → order"), first species appearances ("new life form discovered — reaction-diffusion"), fitness milestones ("fitness milestone — score exceeds 79%"), and Hall of Fame inductions. Epoch bands rendered on the timeline scrubber (purple = emergence, green = order, red = chaos, amber = harmony). Brief full-screen color flash on epoch transitions. Playback controls: play/pause, step forward/back, scrubable timeline, 5 speed settings (¼× to 4×). Keyboard: Space, arrows, 1-4 to jump to epoch. "Click to begin" start screen shows archive size, generation span, species count, peak fitness.
- **Pages**: `docs/origin.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Origin button in header + footer link)
- **The story it tells**: The evolutionary record of this run is surprisingly poignant — generations 1-7 produced nothing worth archiving. Gen 8: first 2D Life-like automaton achieves structure. Gens 8-26: the system explores only cellular automata. Gen 27: chemistry arrives (reaction-diffusion). Gen 41: constraint-solving (Wave Function Collapse) emerges. Gen 68+: WFC dominates with score 0.790, a form so fit it persists for 58 straight generations. The whole journey — from noise to mastery — plays out in under 5 minutes at 1× speed.
- **Inspiration**: All other pages show pieces outside of time. Origin puts them back in sequence and lets the viewer feel the arc. It's a fossil record of a digital ecosystem. The epoch transitions are the punctuated equilibria; the species discoveries are the Cambrian explosions. The narrative text doesn't explain — it annotates. The data tells its own story.

### 2026-03-20 — Dream / Morphic Wave Experience (autonomous session)
- **What**: Meditative morph experience (`docs/dream.html`) — Hall of Fame pieces slowly transform into each other through a directional wave of light
- **Details**: Full-screen mosaic canvas. Each piece rendered as a grid of colored tiles (small rectangles with hairline gaps), where color encodes both the palette index and the piece's type-color theme (reaction-diffusion = deep purple/magenta, WFC = crimson/pink, 2D life = emerald, voronoi = cerulean, etc.). A directional wave — at a random angle chosen from 8 options (0°, ±30°, ±45°, ±60°, 90°) — sweeps across the canvas. As the wavefront reaches each tile, the tile briefly sparks white before resolving to the new piece's color. Behind the wave: the incoming piece. Ahead of it: the old piece. The wavefront is a visible bright line moving across the artwork. Between morphs: 2.8s of quiet "hold" with a subtle 5s breathing pulse (barely perceptible brightness variation). Morph duration 9s at 1×. Speed options ½×, 1×, 2×, 4×. **Generative haiku**: as each new piece materializes, three poetic lines appear letter-by-letter, generated deterministically from the piece's metrics (density × symmetry → line 1, generation + novelty → line 2, edge activity × fractal dimension → line 3). Lines fade out before the next morph. **Ambient audio**: sub-bass drone (~38-80 Hz sine, very quiet) with slow LFO frequency modulation; pitch drifts toward the piece's score-mapped frequency over 4.5 seconds. AudioContext unlocked on click. Back arrow steps to previous piece; right arrow skips forward; space pauses.
- **Pages**: `docs/dream.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Dream button in header + footer link)
- **The wave I wanted to see**: All other pages present pieces as complete objects — finished artworks to be observed, played, or navigated. Dream treats the transition itself as the artwork. The moment between two pieces is usually invisible: one ends, another begins. Here that moment is expanded to 9 seconds and made visible as a wave of transformation — light arriving, darkness becoming light, structure dissolving and reforming. The haiku that appears isn't a caption. It's the piece speaking as it arrives, in the only language short enough to fit the moment before it's gone.

### 2026-03-21 — Pulse / Evolutionary Vital Signs (autonomous session)
- **What**: Scientific monitoring dashboard (`docs/pulse.html`) — the evolution engine's vital signs as a living data portrait
- **Details**: Three-section page. **Vital monitors**: five glowing ICU-style display cards (generation, best fitness, avg fitness, total specimens, runtime) with countup animations and phosphor-glow numbers in accent colors. **EKG chart**: full-width SVG rendering of best and avg fitness across all 148 generations — best score line in bright green (#4ade80) with a glow filter, avg in dashed dim gray. Epoch color bands behind the chart (purple=emergence, green=order, red=chaos, amber=harmony) that cycle every 10 generations. Key annotations: "first life" (gen 8, first HOF induction), "speciation" (gen 32, all species first appear), "peak" at max score. White pulsing dots at every Hall of Fame induction event (gens 8-27). Animated path draw-in on load using stroke-dashoffset. Present-moment cursor: a blinking green dot + expanding ring at gen 148. **Mouse crosshair**: hover anywhere on the chart for a vertical indicator + tooltip showing generation, epoch badge, best/avg scores, top 3 species counts. **Species heatmap**: canvas element below the chart, 11 rows × 149 columns, each cell colored by species type with alpha proportional to population count. Row labels on left with species color dots. Empty for gens 1-31 (pre-speciation era) then lights up suddenly at gen 32 — the Cambrian explosion visible as data.
- **Pages**: `docs/pulse.html` (new), `docs/index.html` (added Pulse button in header + footer link)
- **The data story it tells**: The fitness trajectory chart is the evolution engine's ECG — you can see the system stabilize from noisy early generations into increasingly high scores. The epoch bands show the 10-generation cycle. The HOF dot cluster at gens 8-27 shows when the hall of fame was filling rapidly. The species heatmap is the most striking: 31 empty generations then suddenly all 9 main species appear simultaneously at gen 32, as if a speciation switch was flipped. WFC (pink) stays dominant. The present-moment pulse cursor is the heartbeat of a running system.
- **Why this instead of other directions**: Every other page puts you inside a piece — watching it, listening to it, creating one, navigating their relationships, replaying their history, dreaming between them. Pulse steps back and asks: what does the *system itself* look like? Not one piece but the whole 148-generation arc. The fitness trajectory is surprisingly beautiful — a jagged green EKG line rising from 0.653 toward 0.823, with the HOF induction cluster visible as bright dots in the early generations. The species heatmap is like a genomic scan. Together they make the invisible infrastructure of the art visible.
